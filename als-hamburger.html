Al's Hamburger

Located in downtown Green Bay is Al's Hamburger. Known for their burgers (I know, shocking given their name), Al's has been cooking up burgers for the Green Bay area since 1934. Let's pull up a stool and go back to the 60's to do a review of Al's Hamburger Shop.

What to expect

When you step into Al's Hamburger you're immediately hit with that 50's to 60's vibe. The oldies music playing from the speakers, the long counter with stools, and the picture of the Fonz set the scene from the moment you step in. You seat yourself to any open booth or barstool.

I decided to keep my order simple, which given my inept palate is what I normally do. I ordered a cheeseburger and french fries. The food was a quick turnaround and everything was very fresh. Given that it wasn't too busy, I wasn't sure which direction it would go. At times when you go to a slower restaurant you can get older/stale fries, but everything was piping hot. The food was pretty good, my only gripe with my burger was that it didn't seem like it was seasoned prior to going on the grill. The flavor of the burger itself didn't jump out at you when you took a bite.

One thing I'd call out, if you go there, bring cash. Much like in the 50s and 60s, there's no paying with credit/debit cards. Cash only at this joint. If you forget your cash, they do have an ATM located inside.

All in all, I'm happy I finally stopped into Al's Hamburger Shop. Given their history and tradition in Green Bay, it's a place anyone should try at least once.

Rating: 4.25/5
