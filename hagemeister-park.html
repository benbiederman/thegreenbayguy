Hagemeister Park

Hagemeister Park was the home of the Green Bay Packers during their first two seasons in the National Football League. So how does a restaurant named after one of the most historic sites in Green Bay score when it comes to food? To me, Hagemeister Park is just meh. In my opinion, given the vibe of the restaurant you'd think it'd be a better finished product.

What to expect

Located in beautiful downtown Green Bay, along the Fox River and on the CityDeck is Hagemeister Park. Hagemeister Park has a menu that caters to a wide audience. To compliment their restaurant, they have a couple bars, one located inside and the other located out back along the CityDeck. The outdoor bar is absolutely beautiful, in fact, I ranked Hagemeister Park slightly higher due to that bar. It's beautiful to sit out there on a Summer night and enjoy the atmosphere.

The food on the other hand... not the greatest. When dining at Hagemeister Park for dinner. We almost ordered a menu item but after the waiter confirmed it had onions in it, we ordered something else. Once we got our dish, we started eating only to realize that our dish had onions in it. We asked the waiter about the onions, he stated that it normally doesn't have it but the cooks decided to add them. Then instead of correcting it or offering an alternative dish to eat, he just said he could take a little bit of money off our bill, despite the only things we ordered being the dishes they messed up by putting onions in. Other experiences at Hagemeister havn't been as bad, but this one left a bad taste in my mouth... much like those onions. If it's up to me, I'd rather go to Titletown Brewing or Copper State Brewing for dinner and then walk down to Hagemeister Park for some drinks out by the water.

Rating: 3.5/5
